Key Takeaways

  • AI context is everything: The quality of AI output is directly proportional to the quality of the context it receives. Building a structured “Second Brain” approach to prompting is a key skill for working effectively with AI.
  • Amazon QuickSight Q brings natural language to data analytics, enabling non-technical users to generate insights without writing code — a big step toward democratizing data.
  • CloudFront is more than a CDN: It can serve as a security layer, cost optimization tool, and performance foundation for every workload, not just static assets.
  • Hackathon mindset: The UTMorpho team’s 36-hour sprint showed how structured thinking (problem definition → MVP → iteration) under pressure can produce a working product. Failure and pivoting are part of the process.
  • LLMs are not deterministic even at Temperature=0: Hardware parallelism and floating-point non-determinism mean “deterministic” settings are a useful approximation, not a guarantee. Systems depending on exact reproducibility need additional mitigation strategies.
  • Multi-agent systems for compliance: Using a “Virtual Credit Committee” of specialized agents with guardrails shows how AI can be applied responsibly in high-stakes financial domains.

Personal Experience

Attending the FCAJ Community Day was an inspiring experience. Meeting AWS practitioners, builders, and fellow interns in person — at the iconic Bitexco Financial Tower — made the event feel significant and memorable.

What stood out most was the diversity of the sessions: from foundational AI mindset and cloud infrastructure, to a live hackathon story and cutting-edge multi-agent architecture. Each session gave me a different lens to think about cloud and AI.

The talk on LLM non-determinism was particularly eye-opening for me as someone working with serverless backends — it reminded me that assumptions in software (like “deterministic settings = deterministic output”) often break down at scale or under optimization pressure.

Overall, the event reinforced my motivation to keep building on AWS and to stay connected with the growing FCJ community.
